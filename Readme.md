Abstract

Temporal Coherence Gravity (TCG) is an exploratory research framework investigating whether causality, time, spacetime, and gravitational phenomena may emerge from underlying temporal coherence relationships.

Rather than treating spacetime geometry as fundamental, the framework proposes that coherent temporal organization gives rise to causal structure, which subsequently manifests as emergent spacetime and gravitational behavior.

The current manuscript demonstrates recovery of the weak-field form of General Relativity through an effective coherence-metric mapping and proposes several falsifiable predictions, including potential observational signatures in Gamma Ray Burst timing distributions.

This project is intended as a speculative and testable research framework rather than a replacement for established physical theories.

Key Ideas

The framework explores the possibility that:

Causality may be more fundamental than spacetime geometry.
Temporal coherence may generate emergent causal structure.
Gravitational phenomena may arise from coherence gradients.
Time may be an emergent consequence of causal organization.
Information and coherence relationships may ultimately prove more fundamental than any proposed temporal substrate.
