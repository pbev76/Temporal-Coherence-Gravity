I Killed Timmy

A research note on Temporal Event Quanta, conceptual scaffolding, and preserved information.

Version 0.1 introduced Temporal Event Quanta, informally called “Timmies,” as a conceptual substrate for temporal organization.

What Was Timmy?

In the earliest form of Temporal Coherence Gravity, Temporal Event Quanta were introduced as discrete microscopic entities underlying temporal organization.

The idea was useful because it provided something concrete to reason about: local state, temporal phase, update ordering, coherence, and emergent causality.

In Version 0.1, Timmy served as scaffolding.

Why Kill Timmy?

As the framework developed, it became increasingly clear that most of the important behavior did not depend on TEQs as literal physical objects.

The essential structure appeared to be:

Information → Coherence → Causality → Spacetime

The coherence relationships mattered more than the proposed entity carrying those relationships.

That means Temporal Event Quanta may not be fundamental. They may simply be a useful early model.

What Survives?

The death of Timmy does not destroy the framework.

If anything, it strengthens it.

Removing TEQs shifts the theory from a proposed microscopic particle-like substrate toward a more general informational coherence framework.

The question changes from:

What are the fundamental temporal particles?

to:

What informational relationships generate stable causality and emergent spacetime?

The Irony

If information is fundamental, then Timmy is not truly gone.

Timmy survives as a recorded informational state in the history of the theory.

The entity may be unnecessary. The information remains.

Timmy was killed as a fundamental object, but preserved as information.

Version History

Version 0.1: Introduces Temporal Event Quanta as a conceptual substrate for temporal coherence.

Version 0.2: Planned reformulation investigating whether TEQs can be removed entirely in favor of informational coherence relationships.

Version 0.3: Possible future development exploring whether observable physical laws are best understood as descriptions of stable informational-coherence structures rather than fundamental primitives.

Status

This page is not part of the formal Version 0.1 paper. It is a research-history note documenting the evolution of the framework.

The Version 0.1 manuscript retains TEQs intentionally. Future versions may remove them.

That evolution is part of the scientific process.
